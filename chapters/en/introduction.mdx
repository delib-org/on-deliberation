# Introduction

Modern democracies often fail not because pluralism is impossible, but because coordination is under-designed. The core premise of this book is that conflict, uncertainty, and scale can be mediated through better deliberative structures rather than merely endured. That claim draws on traditions of critical inquiry, institutional design, and bounded cognition [@ostrom1990] [@kahneman2011].

## The Consensus Score

One working heuristic in the manuscript models agreement quality as a function of evaluative strength and group participation:

$$Consensus Score = \bar{E} \times \sqrt{N}$$

The point is not that this specific formula is final. The point is that deliberative systems benefit from transparent scoring rules that can be critiqued in public and improved over time.
